## 2. BUDGET ALLOCATION

### Reverse-Engineering from 15 Leads/Month

| Metric | Russian/Ukrainian Niche | Buyers (Search) | Sellers (Search) | Brand | PMax |
|--------|------------------------|-----------------|-------------------|-------|------|
| Avg CPC | $1.50 | $10.00 | $9.00 | $1.00 | $5.00 |
| Expected CTR | 8% | 4.5% | 5% | 15% | 1.5% |
| Landing Page CVR | 12% | 4% | 6% | 20% | 3% |
| Cost Per Lead | $12.50 | $250.00 | $150.00 | $5.00 | $167.00 |
| Target Leads/Mo | 3 | 5 | 3 | 1 | 3 |
| Monthly Budget | $37.50 | $1,250.00 | $450.00 | $5.00 | $500.00 |

**Note on Niche volume:** Russian/Ukrainian searches are low volume (estimated 50-100 impressions/month across all keywords). We will get every possible click but volume is capped by population size. The budget is small because demand is small, but ROI is extraordinary.

### Final Budget Table

| Campaign | Daily Budget | Monthly Budget | % of Total |
|----------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| GK - Brand | $2.00 | $60.00 | 2.7% |
| GK - Russian/Ukrainian Niche | $5.00 | $150.00 | 6.7% |
| GK - Temecula Valley Buyers | $42.00 | $1,260.00 | 56.0% |
| GK - Temecula Valley Sellers | $17.00 | $510.00 | 22.7% |
| GK - Performance Max - Local | $9.00 | $270.00 | 12.0% |
| **TOTAL** | **$75.00** | **$2,250.00** | **100%** |

**Why $2,250/month:** Real estate in Inland Empire SoCal runs $8-15 CPC for competitive terms. At a blended cost per lead of $150 (mixing cheap niche leads with expensive generic leads), 15 leads requires approximately $2,250. This is a conservative estimate; actual CPL may be lower if Quality Scores are strong and the niche campaigns outperform.

**Niche budget overweighted at $150:** We set this higher than the math requires ($37.50) because we want maximum impression share on these terms. We will never spend the full $150 most days, but on days when multiple Russian-community searches happen, we want to capture every single one. Unspent budget here gets reallocated by Google's daily overspend allowance to other days.

## 3. BID STRATEGY PER CAMPAIGN

| Campaign | Bid Strategy | Rationale |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| GK - Brand | Manual CPC ($2.00 max) | Low volume, defensive. No need for automation. Cap at $2 to avoid wasted spend. |
| GK - Russian/Ukrainian Niche | Manual CPC ($4.00 max) | Too low volume for automated bidding to learn. Manual CPC keeps us aggressive on every auction. |
| GK - Temecula Valley Buyers | Maximize Conversions with tCPA $200 | Enough volume to feed the algorithm. tCPA of $200 gives room to learn while keeping below $250 ceiling. After 30 conversions, tighten to $175. |
| GK - Temecula Valley Sellers | Maximize Conversions with tCPA $150 | Seller leads have higher CVR. Tighter tCPA is justified. Start here, adjust after 15-20 conversions. |
| GK - Performance Max - Local | Maximize Conversion Value | PMax works best with value-based bidding. Since we have conversion values assigned ($50 form, $25 call), let the algorithm optimize for value. |

**Bid Strategy Transition Plan:**
- Weeks 1-2: All campaigns start on Manual CPC to gather data
- Week 3: Switch Buyers and Sellers to Maximize Clicks with bid caps to build conversion data
- Week 4-6: Once each campaign has 10+ conversions, switch to Maximize Conversions
- Week 7+: Add target CPA once 30+ conversions exist
- Brand and Niche stay on Manual CPC permanently due to low volume

- Document every test result in a shared spreadsheet
- Statistical significance threshold: 90% confidence minimum

## KEY STRATEGIC NOTES

**1. The Russian/Ukrainian niche is your unfair advantage.** No one else can compete here. Even at $150/month budget, this campaign will likely produce 3-4 high-quality, high-trust leads. These leads close at 2-3x the rate of generic leads because of cultural trust and language exclusivity.

**2. Seller leads are worth 10x buyer leads in terms of revenue certainty.** A listing generates commission regardless of who buys. Prioritize the Home Value campaign and consider building a dedicated seller landing page as the single highest-leverage improvement George can make.

**3. Budget is conservative.** At $2,250/month, we are buying into competitive auctions at about 35-50% impression share on non-brand terms. If George wants to scale to 25-30 leads/month, budget would need to increase to $4,000-5,000/month. The current budget is designed for capital efficiency, not market dominance.

**4. Quality Score is everything at this budget level.** With limited budget, we cannot afford to waste money on low QS keywords. If any keyword drops below QS 5, either improve the landing page experience, tighten ad copy relevance, or pause it.

**5. The biggest risk is conversion tracking inaccuracy.** If forms or call tracking are not recording properly, the automated bid strategies will make bad decisions. Verify conversion tracking every week for the first month.

**6. PMax is a wildcard.** It will likely show ads on YouTube, Display, and Gmail in addition to Search and Maps. Monitor asset group performance closely. If PMax starts cannibalizing branded search impressions (common), add brand terms as negative keywords at the account level for PMax (using the brand exclusion list feature).
