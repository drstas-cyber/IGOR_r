Top Mistakes Sellers Make

Avoid these common pitfalls that cost Temecula Valley homeowners thousands of dollars and add weeks to their selling timeline.

1

Overpricing the Home

The most expensive mistake a seller can make. Overpriced homes sit on the market, develop a stigma, and often sell for less than they would have at the correct price. Buyers and agents track days on market closely — an overpriced listing signals desperation when you finally reduce the price.

2

Skipping Necessary Repairs

Buyers notice deferred maintenance immediately. Leaky faucets, cracked tiles, peeling paint, and worn flooring signal that the home has not been well cared for. These issues almost always come up during inspections and give buyers leverage to negotiate your price down significantly.

3

Poor Quality Photos

Over 95% of buyers start their search online. Dark, blurry, or poorly composed listing photos mean your home gets scrolled past in seconds. Professional HDR photography is not a luxury — it is essential for making a strong first impression and generating showings.

4

Wrong Timing

While the Temecula market is currently a seller's market, timing still matters. Spring and early summer typically see the highest buyer activity, but local factors like school calendars, interest rates, and inventory levels all play a role. George helps you identify the optimal window for your situation.

5

Not Staging the Home

Buyers need to envision themselves living in your space. Cluttered, overly personalized, or empty rooms make that difficult. Proper staging — even basic decluttering and rearranging — can increase your sale price by 1-5% and significantly reduce time on market.

How to Maximize Your Sale Price

Smart preparation can add thousands to your bottom line. Here are the strategies that deliver the highest return for Temecula Valley sellers.

Boost Curb Appeal

First impressions happen before the front door opens. Fresh landscaping, a power-washed driveway, a painted front door, and clean exterior surfaces create an inviting first impression. In the Temecula Valley's sunny climate, drought-tolerant landscaping with strategic color plantings makes a strong impact.

Declutter & Depersonalize

Remove excess furniture to make rooms feel larger. Pack away personal photos, collections, and unique decor. Buyers need to imagine their own family in your home. Clean closets and organized storage areas signal that the home has ample space — something every buyer values.

Minor Upgrades That Pay Off

Not all upgrades deliver equal returns. Focus on high-impact, low-cost improvements: fresh interior paint in neutral tones, updated cabinet hardware, modern light fixtures, and new outlet covers. Kitchen and bathroom updates consistently deliver the highest ROI for Temecula Valley sellers.

Timing the Market

The best time to list depends on your specific circumstances, but spring through early summer typically sees the most buyer activity in the Temecula Valley. George monitors market conditions weekly and will advise you on the optimal listing date to capture peak buyer demand for your neighborhood.

Understanding the Costs of Selling

Knowing what to expect financially helps you plan effectively and avoid surprises at closing. Here is a breakdown of typical selling costs in Temecula Valley.

Agent Commission

Typically 5-6% of the sale price, split between the listing agent and buyer's agent. This covers all marketing, negotiation, and transaction management. On a $740K home, this ranges from $37,000 to $44,400. George's commission covers his full-service marketing package with no hidden fees.

Closing Costs

Sellers typically pay 1-3% of the sale price in closing costs, which includes escrow fees, title insurance, transfer taxes, and recording fees. In Riverside County, this usually totals between $7,400 and $22,200 on a median-priced home.

Pre-Sale Repairs

Budget $2,000-$10,000 for necessary repairs and improvements before listing. George provides a prioritized list of repairs that will deliver the highest return, so you invest wisely rather than over-improving for your market.

Staging Costs

Professional staging typically costs $1,500-$5,000 depending on the size of your home and scope of staging needed. For occupied homes, George provides complimentary staging recommendations that can achieve similar results at minimal cost.
